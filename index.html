01 — Concept

We create artifacts of a city that never existed.

Not a beginning. Just something that remained.

No one stays. Still, something passes.

It wasn’t light. What it left behind.

Nothing happens here. That’s why it stays.

After everything ends, something remains.

02 — The Works

Each work is not an image of Tokyo.
It is a fragment of a city that never existed.

They do not document reality.
They suggest something that might have been there.

Subtle, but persistent.
Quiet, but irreversible.

What appears is not presence,
but residue.

Ghost — Residual Tokyo
Chemical — Synthetic Tokyo
03 — Implementation

What happens

Guests do not just stay.
They encounter a city that never existed.

Each room becomes a different memory of Tokyo.
The hotel transforms from a place to stay into a place to experience an alternative reality.

Installation

The project is implemented as a layered system:

  • • Anchor: A central work defining the space
  • • Variation: Multiple works distributed across rooms
  • • Transition: Subtle elements that appear in between

In addition, fragments appear throughout the space, sometimes in the form of small cards.
Not as explanation, but as traces of the experience.

Pilot

The project can begin on a small scale:

  • • 1 floor
  • • 3–5 works
  • • Optional card-based elements

Flexible depending on space and concept.

This is not an art installation.

It is a way to redefine how a hotel is experienced.

In a city like Tokyo, where everything already exists, what remains valuable is what has never existed.

This project introduces a layer of memory that cannot be replicated elsewhere.

This aligns with hotels that position themselves as cultural platforms, not just places to stay.

Designed for a hotel that values culture, not just stay.
